370  /*
371  Several (very different) usages make the spez non trivial
372  The final goal is to obtain a sequence generator of the factor
373  samples, several routes are possible depending on the algorithms:
374 
375  1.- URNG -> Sequence Gen -> CopulaInversion
376  e.g.: CopulaInversion(RandomSequenceGenerator<MersenneTwisterRNG>)
377  2.- PseudoRSG ------------> CopulaInversion
378  e.g.: CopulaInversion(SobolRSG)
379  3.- URNG -> SpecificMapping -> Sequence Gen (bypasses the copula
380  for performance)
381  e.g.: RandomSequenceGenerator<BoxMullerGaussianRng<
382  MersenneTwisterRNG> >
383 
384  Notice that the order the three algorithms involved (uniform gen,
385  sequence construction, distribution mapping) is not always the same.
386  (in fact there could be some other ways to generate but these are
387  the ones in the library now.)
388  Difficulties arise when wanting to use situation 3.- whith a generic
389  RNG, leaving it unspecified
390 
391  Derived classes might specialize (on the copula
392  type) to another type of generator if a more efficient algorithm
393  that the distribution inversion is available; rewritig then the
394  nextSequence method for a particular copula implementation.
395  Some combinations of generators might make no sense, while it
396  could be possible to block template classes corresponding to those
397  cases its not done (yet?) (e.g. a BoxMuller under a TCopula.)
398  Dimensionality coherence (between the generator and the copula)
399  should have been checked by the client code.
400  In multithread usage the sequence generator is expect to be already
401  in position.
402  To sample the latent variable itself users should call
403  LatentModel::latentVarValue with these samples.
404  */
